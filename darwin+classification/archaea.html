Archaea 古细菌

Archaea constitute a domain of single-celled organisms. These microorganisms lack cell nuclei and are therefore prokaryotes. Archaea were initially classified as bacteria, receiving the name archaebacteria (in the Archaebacteria kingdom), but this term has fallen out of use. Archaeal cells have unique properties separating them from the other two domains, Bacteria and Eukaryota. A lot organisms in archaea have the ability to live in conditions that do not suit for normal organisms such as acidic marsh, vocanic vents, etc. Archaea are further divided into multiple recognized phyla. Classification is difficult because most have not been isolated in a laboratory and have been detected only by their gene sequences in environmental samples.

古细菌构成了一个单细胞生物的域。这些微生物缺乏细胞核，因此是原核生物。古细菌最初被归类为细菌，获得了古细菌的名称（在古细菌界）。 但这个术语已不再使用。古细菌细胞具有独特的特性，将它们与其他两个领域分开细菌和真核生物。 古细菌中的许多生物有能力生活在不适合普通生物的条件下如酸性沼泽,高盐地区，火山口等。 古细菌被进一步划分为多个公认的门类。分类是困难的，因为大多数没有在实验室中分离出来，只是通过它们在环境样品中的基因序列来检测。
